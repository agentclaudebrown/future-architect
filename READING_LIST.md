## START HERE — The Intellectual Lineage

### 1. Douglas Engelbart — Augmenting Human Intellect (1962)
**Why:** The original. Engelbart asked what it means to use computers to augment human thinking rather than replace it. He even uses an architect as his example. Your paper is a contemporary answer to his question — 60 years later.
**Read:** https://web.stanford.edu/class/history34q/readings/Engelbart/Engelbart_AugmentIntellect.html
**PDF alternative:** https://www.dougengelbart.org/pubs/papers/scanned/Doug_Engelbart-AugmentingHumanIntellect.pdf
**Note:** Skip to Section III if Section II gets dense — Engelbart himself recommends this.

### 2. Vannevar Bush — As We May Think (1945)
**Why:** Bush imagined the Memex — a machine that stores and associates knowledge the way the human mind does. The continuity argument — same ideas, different substrate — has an 80-year history. Bush is the beginning of it.
**Read:** https://web.mit.edu/sts.035/www/PDFs/think.pdf
**Alternative:** https://www.math.cmu.edu/~af1p/Teaching/INFONET/Papers/AsWeMayThink/bush.html

### 3. Andrej Karpathy — Software 2.0 (2017)
**Why:** Neural networks aren't just a tool — they're a new programming paradigm. Software 1.0 = explicit instructions. Software 2.0 = learned behaviour. Software 3.0 = natural language as code.
**Read:** https://karpathy.medium.com/software-2-0-a64152b37c35
**Software 3.0 explainer:** https://medium.com/@ben_pouladian/andrej-karpathy-on-software-3-0-software-in-the-age-of-ai-b25533da93b6

## THE CRAFT MODELS

### 4. Dario Amodei — Machines of Loving Grace (2024)
**Why:** Your paper is in the same genre. Study the structure. Notice how he handles uncertainty.
**Read:** https://darioamodei.com/essay/machines-of-loving-grace

### 5. Dario Amodei — The Adolescence of Technology (2026)
**Why:** The follow-up. Shows how to write about risk without tipping into panic.
**Read:** https://www.darioamodei.com/essay/the-adolescence-of-technology

### 6. Paul Graham — Keep Your Identity Small (2009)
**Why:** ~800 words. One argument, zero waste. The discipline your paper needs.
**Read:** https://www.paulgraham.com/identity.html

### 7. Jeff Bezos — 2002 API Mandate (internal memo)
**Why:** Short, blunt, changed the architecture of an entire company. Early articulation of agent contracts.
**Read:** https://nordicapis.com/the-bezos-mandate/
**Context:** https://apievangelist.com/2012/01/12/the-secret-to-amazons-success-internal-apis/

## THE IDEAS UNDERNEATH YOUR ARGUMENT

### 8. Nicholas Carr — Is Google Making Us Stupid? (The Atlantic, 2008)
**Why:** The counterargument. Tools reshape cognition, sometimes destructively. Your paper needs to acknowledge this.
**Read:** https://www.theatlantic.com/magazine/archive/2008/07/is-google-making-us-stupid/306868/

### 9. Richard Feynman — Cargo Cult Science (1974)
**Why:** The difference between the appearance of rigour and actual rigour. Read this last.
**Read:** https://calteches.library.caltech.edu/51/2/CargoCult.htm

## THE METHODOLOGY AND ENTERPRISE ANGLE

### 10. Geoffrey Moore — Crossing the Chasm (1991)
**Why:** Why good ideas die in the gap between early adopters and mainstream enterprise. Methodology is the bridge.
**Buy/borrow:** Standard business book. Read first 3 chapters.

### 11. Clayton Christensen — The Innovator's Dilemma (1997)
**Why:** Disruption comes from a different model, not a better version of the same thing.
**Buy/borrow:** Standard business book. Read intro + Chapter 1.

## THE ACCOUNTABILITY AND LEGAL ANGLE

### 13. EU AI Act — Title III + Article 14 (Human Oversight)
**Why:** Article 14 is the statutory basis for the architect role. Enforceable August 2, 2026. Penalties: €35M or 7% of turnover.
**Read:** https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/the-act/
**Article 14 specifically:** https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/article/14/
**Best plain-English analysis:** https://www.zartis.com/ai-governance-accountability-whos-liable-when-your-ai-agent-breaks-something/

### 14. Frank Pasquale — The Black Box Society (Introduction)
**Why:** Algorithmic accountability — the philosophical backbone of the "throat to choke" argument.
**Buy/borrow:** Harvard University Press. Read Introduction only.

### 15. Singapore IMDA — Model AI Governance Framework for Agentic AI (2025)
**Why:** Introduces "Meaningful Human Control (MHC)" — the clearest definition of what the human orchestrator provides.
**Read:** https://www.imda.gov.sg/resources/press-releases-factsheets-and-speeches/press-releases/2025/singapore-releases-advisory-guidelines-on-use-of-personal-data-in-ai-recommendation-and-decision-systems
